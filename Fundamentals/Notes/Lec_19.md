# Browser Software

- Browser software refers to applications or programs that allow users to access and navigate the internet.
- These software applications interpret HTML code, display web pages, and allow users to interact with web content. Some of the most popular browsers include:
- Google Chrome
- Mozilla Firefox
- Microsoft Edge
- Apple Safari
- Opera etc..

# Browser Software cont..

- Google Chrome: Developed by Google, Chrome is one of the most widely used browsers globally. It offers a clean interface, fast performance, and a variety of features such as tabbed browsing, bookmarks, and extensions.
- Mozilla Firefox: Firefox is an open-source browser developed by the Mozilla Foundation. It is known for its customizable interface, privacy features, and support for add-ons and extensions.
- Microsoft Edge: Edge is a web browser developed by Microsoft and is the default browser for Windows 10 and later versions. It is built on the Chromium engine, the same engine that powers Google Chrome, and offers features such as built-in security tools and integration with Microsoft services.

# Browser Software cont..

- Apple Safari: Safari is the default browser for Apple devices, including Mac computers, iPhones, and iPads. It is known for its speed and energy efficiency, as well as features such as Reading List and iCloud integration.
- Opera: Opera is a lesser-known browser that offers a range of features such as built-in ad blocking, VPN support, and a customizable interface. It is available for various operating systems, including Windows, macOS, and Linux.

- Email (Electronic Mail) is a method of exchanging digital messages over the Internet.
- It is one of the oldest and most widely used services of the Internet.
- Messages can include text, images, files, and hyperlinks.
- Popular Email Services
- Gmail
- Outlook
- Yahoo Mail etc..

# How does Email Works?

How does Email Works?

- Sender writes a message using an email client (like Gmail, Outlook).
- Email is sent to an SMTP Server (Simple Mail Transfer Protocol).
- Email travels through the Internet to the recipient's Mail Server.
- Recipient reads the email using POP3/IMAP protocols.

# Email Components

- To: Main recipient(s).
- CC (Carbon Copy): For sending copies to others.
- BCC (Blind Carbon Copy): Sends a copy secretly.
- Subject: Brief title of the message.
- Body: Main message content.
- Attachments: Files sent with the email (e.g., PDFs, images)

# Common Email Protocols

## SMTP (Simple Mail Transfer Protocol)
- ☑ Used For: Sending emails from a client to a mail server or between servers.
- ☑ Works with POP3 or IMAP to deliver the message to the recipient.
- ☑ Without SMTP, you can't send emails.

## POP3 (Post Office Protocol Version 3)
- ☑ Used For: Receiving emails by downloading them from the mail server to your device.
- ☑ Messages are usually deleted from the server after download.
- ☑ Good for offline access.
- ☑ Not ideal for using multiple devices.

# Common Email Protocols cont..

## IMAP (Internet Message Access Protocol)

- ☑ Used For: Accessing emails while keeping them stored on the server.
- ☑ Syncs your inbox across multiple devices.
- ☑ Supports folders and email status (read/unread).
- ☑ Needs internet to work properly

# What is a spam filter?

- A spam filter is a program or service that is designed to identify and block unsolicited and unwanted email messages, commonly known as "spam."

# How does Spam Filter Work?

## Blacklisting:
- A blacklist is a list of email addresses, domains, or IP addresses that are known to send spam.
- Spam filters can use blacklists to block email messages from these sources.

## Content filtering:
- Spam filters can analyze the content of email messages to identify spam-like characteristics, such as excessive use of capital letters, misleading subject lines, and suspicious links.

# How does Spam Filter Work? cont.

## Sender authentication :

- Spam filters can check the sender's domain and use various authentication techniques, such as Sender Policy Framework (SPF) and DomainKeys Identified Mail (DKIM), to verify that the sender is legitimate and not spoofing the email address.

## Machine learning :

- Spam filters can use machine learning algorithms to analyze large amounts of data and identify patterns in spam messages. Machine learning can help improve the accuracy of spam filters over time.

# Difference between CC and BCC

CC and BCC are both ways to send a copy of an email message to additional recipients. However, they serve different purposes:

- CC (Carbon Copy):
- When you CC someone on an email, it means that you are sending a copy of the email to another person for their information or reference.
- The person who is CC'd on an email is visible to all other recipients, and their email address is included in the message header.

# Difference between CC and BCC cont..

## BCC (Blind Carbon Copy):

- When you BCC someone on an email, it means that you are sending a copy of the email to another person without other recipients knowing that the person is included.
- The person who is BCC'd on an email is not visible to other recipients, and their email address is not included in the message header.
- The person who is BCC'd is not the primary recipient of the message, and they are not expected to take any action in response to the email.

# Difference between Email Client and Web based Email Service

## Email Client
- An email client is a computer program or application that is used to access and manage email messages.
- An email client is installed on a local device, such as a computer or mobile phone, and communicates with an email server to send and receive email messages.
- Examples of email clients include Microsoft Outlook, Apple Mail, Mozilla Thunderbird, and many others.

## Web based Email Service
- A web-based email service, on the other hand, is an email service that can be accessed through a web browser.
- Examples of web-based email services include Gmail, Yahoo Mail, Outlook.com, and many others.

# What is a phishing scam?

- Phishing is a type of cyber attack where scammers use fraudulent emails or messages to trick individuals into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or other personal information.
- The goal of phishing scams is to steal information, money or install malicious software on the victim's device.
