Great question — let me look at only the **Semester Final** papers (2021, 2022, 2023, 2024, 2024 1st) to give you a realistic picture of what a single final exam looks like.Switch to the **"Year-by-year table"** tab to see exactly how marks were distributed in each paper.

The big picture from 5 semester finals:

**Almost guaranteed (high) — ~10–14 marks each:**

- Continuity & differentiability (~13 marks, appeared in all 5)
- Beta & gamma functions (~9 marks, appeared in all 5)
- Integration (~6–9 marks, appeared in 4/5)
- Leibnitz / successive differentiation (~6 marks, appeared in 4/5)

**Usually there (medium) — ~5–8 marks:**

- Maxima & minima, Volume of revolution, Wallis formula, Taylor/Maclaurin series, Graph sketching

**Occasionally there (low) — ~2–4 marks:**

- Mean value theorem, Linear approximation, Polar/arc length, Radius of curvature, Related rates

So realistically, if you master the top 5 topics you can target **roughly 50–55 marks out of ~80** in a semester final — that's the safe scoring zone.
