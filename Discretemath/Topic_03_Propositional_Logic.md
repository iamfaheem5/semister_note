## Study Notes
- **Truth tables for tautologies/contradictions** appear every single year. Practice these specific forms:
  - (p ∧ q) ∧ ¬(p ∨ q) [contradiction]
  - ¬(p ∧ q) ↔ (¬p ∨ ¬q) [De Morgan / equivalence]
  - [(p ∨ q) ∧ (p → r) ∧ (q → r)] → r [tautology]
- **Laws of logic** are used to simplify/verify equivalences. Memorize: De Morgan's, distributive, associative, commutative, absorption, identity, negation, double negation.
- **Converse/Inverse/Contrapositive** definitions are tested with example sentences.
- **Proposition identification** is a recurring warm-up question. Know that a proposition must be a declarative sentence with a definite truth value.
