# Set Theory

> **Priority: HIGH** | Appears every year (2018-2024)  
> **Frequency:** ~22 sub-question appearances across 7 years  
> **Related topics:** Relations, Graphs, Venn Diagrams, Principle of Inclusion-Exclusion

---

## Core Concepts Tested
- Definition of set, examples, laws of set theory
- Venn diagrams for union, intersection, complement, difference
- Shading regions in Venn diagrams (A ∩ Bᶜ, (B/A)ᶜ, etc.)
- Principle of Inclusion and Exclusion (PIE)
- Cardinality formula: |A ∪ B| = |A| + |B| - |A ∩ B|
- Numerical PIE problems with 2 or 3 sets

## Study Notes
- **Venn diagram shading** is guaranteed almost every year. Common targets:
  - A ∩ Bᶜ
  - (B / A)ᶜ  (or B\A complement)
  - A' ∪ B'
  - (A ∪ B)' = A' ∩ B' (De Morgan's law — tested in 2024 with proof)
- **PIE theorem** is frequently asked for proof. Also, numerical 3-set problems are common (80 students / 60 people variations).
- The exact cardinality formula |A ∪ B| = |A| + |B| - |A ∩ B| was explicitly asked to prove in 2020.
- Laws of set theory (commutative, associative, distributive, De Morgan's, identity, complement, etc.) are asked for listing.
