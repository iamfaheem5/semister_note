## Study Notes
- **Translation exercises are now a staple.** The "lions" and "friends/perfect" translations have repeated with minor variations.
- Common patterns to master:
  - "No one is perfect" → ¬∃x P(x) or ∀x ¬P(x)
  - "All lions are fierce" → ∀x (P(x) → Q(x))
  - "Some lions don't drink coffee" → ∃x (P(x) ∧ ¬R(x))
  - "All your friends are perfect" → ∀x (F(x) → P(x))
  - "At least one of your friends is perfect" → ∃x (F(x) ∧ P(x))
  - "Not everyone is perfect" → ¬∀x P(x) or ∃x ¬P(x)
- **Truth value evaluation** is straightforward: substitute numbers and check predicate truth.
