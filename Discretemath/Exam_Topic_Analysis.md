## Key Observations

### Must-Prepare Topics (Appear Every Year)
1. **Relations** — reflexive, symmetric, transitive, equivalence, partial order, Hasse diagrams, relation matrices, digraphs, composite relations.
2. **Graph Theory** — definitions (graph, subgraph, bipartite, complete, directed), adjacency/incidence matrices, Euler path/circuit, isomorphism, trees, spanning trees, Prim's algorithm, Handshaking theorem, Euler's formula V-E+R=2.
3. **Propositional Logic** — tautology, contradiction, logical equivalence, truth tables, laws of logic, converse/inverse/contrapositive, conditional statements.
4. **Set Theory** — laws of set theory, Venn diagrams, shading sets, inclusion-exclusion principle.
5. **Recurrence Relations** — recursion definition, obtaining recurrence from closed form, solving homogeneous recurrence (characteristic equation), substitution method.

### Rising Trends
- **Predicate Logic & Quantifiers** appeared prominently in 2021, 2023, 2024. Expect continued emphasis.
- **Combinatorics / Counting / Probability** had a massive spike in **2024** (entire Q3: Pigeonhole, permutations, probability, binomial expansion). This is likely the new major topic area.
- **Graph Theory** questions are becoming more varied (spanning trees, Prim's algorithm, Euler's formula, Handshaking theorem added in recent years).

### Declining / Rare Topics
- **Algebraic Structures** (semigroups, monoids, groups, rings, ideals) appeared only in **2018** and has not returned. Low priority unless syllabus changes.
- **Number Theory** (congruence modulo) appeared only in **2023**.

## Exact Frequently-Repeated Questions

### 1. Recurrence Relation Solution (appears 6 out of 7 years)
> **"Find the solution to the recurrence relation aₙ = 6aₙ₋₁ - 11aₙ₋₂ + 6aₙ₋₃ with the initial conditions a₀ = 2, a₁ = 5 and a₂ = 15."**
- **Years:** 2019, 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023, 2024
- **Frequency:** 6 times

### 2. Define Reflexive, Symmetric, Transitive (appears every year)
> **"Define reflexive, symmetric and transitive relation. Consider the following five relations on the set A = {1, 2, 3, 4} ... Determine whether each is reflexive, symmetric, transitive."**
- **Years:** 2019, 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023, 2024
- **Frequency:** 6+ times

### 3. Recursion / f(4) Problem (appears 5 out of 7 years)
> **"What is recursion? A function f is defined recursively by f(0) = 3, f(n+1) = 2f(n) + 3. Find the value of f(4)."**
- **Years:** 2019, 2020, 2021, 2022, 2024
- **Frequency:** 5 times

### 4. Recurrence Relation from G(k) (appears 4 out of 7 years)
> **"What is recurrence relation? Obtain the recurrence relation for: G(k) = 2.4ᵏ - 5(-3)ᵏ."**
- **Years:** 2018, 2019, 2020, 2022
- **Frequency:** 4 times

### 5. Venn Diagram Shading (appears almost every year)
> **"Consider Universal set U and two sets A and B. Shade the following sets using Venn diagram: i) A ∩ Bᶜ ii) (B / A)ᶜ"**
- **Years:** 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023, 2024
- **Frequency:** 5+ times

### 6. Graph Definitions (appears 4 out of 7 years)

- **Years:** 2019, 2020, 2022, 2024

### 7. Tautology / Contradiction by Truth Table (appears every year)
> Various truth table proofs for tautologies and contradictions.
- **Years:** 2018-2024 (all years)
- **Frequency:** 7+ times

### 8. Principle of Inclusion and Exclusion (appears 4 out of 7 years)
> Various statements/proofs and numerical applications.
- **Years:** 2018, 2019, 2020, 2021, 2023
- **Frequency:** 5 times

### 9. Graph Isomorphism (appears 4 out of 7 years)

- **Years:** 2019, 2020, 2022, 2024

## Mark Distribution Insight

Most questions are structured as:
- **Definition + Example:** 2-3 marks
- **Proof / Verification:** 3-4 marks
- **Numerical Problem / Construction:** 4-6 marks
- **Graph Drawing / Truth Table:** 4-6 marks

Total marks per paper: **70**  
Total questions to answer: **5 out of 6 or 7** (varies by year)

## Recommended Study Priority

1. **Relations & Properties** — Master relation matrices, digraphs, Hasse diagrams, composite relations, reflexive/symmetric/transitive checks.
2. **Graph Theory** — Memorize all graph definitions with figures. Practice adjacency/incidence matrices, Euler path/circuit, trees, spanning trees, Prim's algorithm.
3. **Propositional Logic** — Master truth tables, logical equivalences, laws of logic, converse/contrapositive/inverse.
4. **Recurrence Relations** — Practice solving the exact recurrence that repeats every year (aₙ = 6aₙ₋₁ - 11aₙ₋₂ + 6aₙ₋₃).
5. **Set Theory** — Practice Venn diagram shading and inclusion-exclusion theorem.
6. **Combinatorics / Counting** — NEW priority for 2024+. Pigeonhole principle, permutations with repeated elements, basic probability, binomial theorem.
7. **Predicate Logic & Quantifiers** — Translation exercises are now standard.
8. **Rules of Inference** — Practice identifying rules and constructing valid arguments.
9. **Functions** — Composition, injective/surjective/bijective definitions.
10. **Matrices** — Zero-one matrix join/meet, relation matrices, adjacency matrices.
