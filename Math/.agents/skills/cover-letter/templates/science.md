# Science Cover Letter

> Snapshot for the Science family (Science, Science Advances, Science Translational Medicine, Science Signaling, Science Immunology, Science Robotics). Cover letter expectations parallel Nature; the bar for "broad significance" is comparable.

## Tone

- One-step beyond Nature in significance framing: the work must matter for a general scientific audience, not just a subfield.
- Confident, specific, no marketing adjectives.

## Opening

- State the central advance in the first sentence. The advance, not the topic.
- Identify the broader scientific question that motivates the work in one clause.

## Contribution Framing

- One or two sentences naming what was done.
- One or two sentences naming the headline result with a concrete quantitative anchor that traces to the manuscript.
- One sentence naming what this enables that was previously not possible.

## Journal Fit

- Reference Science's editorial priority on broad reach. Connect this work to that.
- If the work is more specialized (e.g., chemistry methodology), Science Advances may be a better target — flag this honestly rather than overstretching.

## Declarations (Required for Science)

- Originality and exclusivity statements.
- Competing interests: explicit declaration even when "none."
- Data and materials availability: required for all Science submissions.
- IRB / IACUC approval for studies involving humans or animals.

## Length

- Hard ceiling: 350 words.

## Common Desk-Reject Triggers

- "Significant" or "important" used as adjectives without a comparator.
- Specialist work pitched to Science when Science Advances is a better fit.
- Missing data-availability statement (Science requires it explicitly).
