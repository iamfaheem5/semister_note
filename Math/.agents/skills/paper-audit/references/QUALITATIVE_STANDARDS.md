# Qualitative Research Standards Reference

This reference provides assessment criteria for qualitative and mixed-methods research papers. Read this when the paper under review uses qualitative methodology (interviews, ethnography, grounded theory, case study, phenomenology, narrative analysis, etc.).

## When to Apply

Apply these standards when the paper's methodology section describes:
- Qualitative data collection (interviews, observations, focus groups, document analysis)
- Qualitative data analysis (coding, thematic analysis, grounded theory, discourse analysis)
- Mixed methods with a qualitative component
- Theoretical or conceptual papers building on qualitative evidence

Do NOT apply to purely quantitative or computational papers.

## SRQR-Based Assessment (Standards for Reporting Qualitative Research)

The following items are adapted from the SRQR checklist (O'Brien et al., 2014). Not every item applies to every paper — use judgment based on the specific methodology.

### Research Design Transparency

| Item | What to Check | Red Flag |
|------|--------------|----------|
| Paradigm statement | Is the philosophical/epistemological stance stated? | Missing entirely in a methods-heavy qualitative paper |
| Method justification | Why was this qualitative approach chosen over alternatives? | "We used qualitative methods" with no further justification |
| Researcher positioning | Does the author acknowledge their relationship to the topic/participants? | No reflexivity statement in sensitive research |

### Sampling & Data Collection

| Item | What to Check | Red Flag |
|------|--------------|----------|
| Sampling strategy | Is the sampling approach described and justified? (purposive, theoretical, snowball, etc.) | "We interviewed 15 participants" with no sampling rationale |
| Participant description | Are participant characteristics adequately described? | No demographic or contextual information |
| Data saturation | Is there evidence or discussion of data saturation? | No mention of when/why data collection stopped |
| Data collection tools | Are interview guides, observation protocols, or other tools described? | "Semi-structured interviews were conducted" with no further detail |
| Data collection context | Are setting, timing, and duration described? | No contextual information about data collection |

### Data Analysis

| Item | What to Check | Red Flag |
|------|--------------|----------|
| Analysis approach | Is the specific analysis method named and described? | "Data were analyzed qualitatively" (too vague) |
| Coding process | Are coding stages described (open, axial, selective / initial, focused)? | "We used NVivo for coding" with no process detail |
| Code examples | Are example codes or themes illustrated with data excerpts? | Themes presented without supporting evidence |
| Analyst involvement | How many researchers coded? Was there intercoder reliability? | Single coder with no verification strategy |
| Iteration description | How did analysis evolve? Were codes revised? | Linear process description with no iteration |

### Trustworthiness & Rigor

| Strategy | Description | When Expected |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Member checking | Participants reviewed findings | Strongly expected for interview-based studies |
| Triangulation | Multiple data sources, methods, or analysts | Expected for complex phenomena |
| Audit trail | Documentation of analytical decisions | Expected for grounded theory and case studies |
| Peer debriefing | External review of analysis process | Good practice, not always required |
| Negative case analysis | Deliberately seeking disconfirming evidence | Expected for theory-building studies |
| Thick description | Rich contextual detail enabling transferability | Expected for ethnographic and case study work |

### Reflexivity Assessment

Reflexivity is the researcher's critical self-examination of their role in the research process.

**Strong reflexivity** includes:
- Acknowledgment of the researcher's background, assumptions, and potential biases
- Discussion of how researcher positionality may have influenced data collection and interpretation
- Description of strategies used to manage researcher influence

**Weak reflexivity** (flag as issue):
- Token statement ("The researcher acknowledges potential bias") with no specifics
- Complete absence of reflexivity in sensitive or politically charged research
- No discussion of how the researcher's role (insider/outsider) shaped access and interpretation

## Severity Calibration for Qualitative Papers

| Issue | Severity | Rationale |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| No coding process described | Major | Reviewers cannot assess analytical rigor |
| No saturation discussion | Moderate | Common omission, but important for credibility |
| Missing reflexivity | Moderate (sensitive topics) / Minor (neutral topics) | Depends on research context |
| No member checking | Minor | One of many trustworthiness strategies |
| Vague sampling rationale | Moderate | Threatens transferability claims |
| Single coder, no verification | Moderate | Weakens analytical trustworthiness |
| No triangulation | Minor-Moderate | Depends on claims made |
