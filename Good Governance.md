### **1. Define good governance. (2 Marks)**

Good governance means managing the country in a fair, honest, and effective way. It is the proper use of government power and public resources to serve the people. It includes transparency, accountability, rule of law, and public participation. The main goal is to build trust between the government and citizens and improve everyone’s life.

---

### **2. Write down the elements of good governance. (3–5 Marks)**

The main elements are:

*   **Accountability:** Government officers and institutions must answer for their actions and decisions.
*   **Transparency:** Government information and decisions should be open and easy for the public to see.
*   **Rule of Law:** Laws must be fair, and everyone must follow them equally.
*   **Participation:** Ordinary people should take part in making decisions that affect their lives.
*   **Responsiveness:** Government must act quickly to solve public problems and needs.
*   **Equity and Inclusiveness:** All people—rich or poor, men or women—must get fair treatment and equal opportunities.
*   **Effectiveness and Efficiency:** Government work should give good results without wasting time or money.
*   **Consensus Oriented:** Decisions should consider the interests of different groups in society.

---

### **3. How can good governance be established in a developing country like Bangladesh? (3–5 Marks)**

Good governance can be improved in Bangladesh through these steps:

*   **Strengthen Democracy:** Hold free and fair elections so that leaders truly represent the people.
*   **Reduce Corruption:** Apply strict laws and punish corrupt officials in government offices.
*   **Digital Government:** Expand e-governance and online services so people do not need to visit offices many times.
*   **Empower Local Government:** Give more power and money to Union Parishads and municipalities so they can solve local problems.
*   **Ensure Transparency:** Use the Right to Information (RTI) Act so citizens can ask for government records.
*   **Reform Bureaucracy:** Simplify rules, reduce paperwork, and train civil servants to be more citizen-friendly.
*   **Judicial Independence:** Ensure courts can work freely without political pressure.
*   **Public Participation:** Involve citizens and local groups in planning and development projects.

---

### **4. Briefly discuss the drawbacks of ensuring good governance. (3–5 Marks)**

There are many challenges in ensuring good governance:

*   **Corruption:** Bribery and dishonesty in public offices slow down development and reduce public trust.
*   **Political Interference:** Politicians often control administration and recruitment, which weakens fair decision-making.
*   **Bureaucratic Red Tape:** Too many complex rules and delays make simple tasks difficult for common people.
*   **Weak Accountability:** Many officials do not face punishment for mistakes or misuse of power.
*   **Poverty and Illiteracy:** Poor and uneducated people cannot easily demand their rights or participate in governance.
*   **Overpopulation:** A large population puts huge pressure on limited resources and public services.
*   **Institutional Weakness:** Many government bodies lack proper funding, skilled staff, and modern technology.
*   **Lack of Rule of Law:** Sometimes laws are not applied equally, especially to powerful or rich people.
