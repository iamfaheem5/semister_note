### 1. What is local government? *(2 marks)*

Local government is the system that runs public affairs at the village, town, or district level. It is a body elected or set up by the people of a small area to solve local problems like roads, water supply, schools, and markets. It works under the central government but looks after the daily needs of the local people.

---

### 2. Classify local government. *(3 marks)*

In Bangladesh, local government is mainly divided into **two** types:

*   **Rural Local Government** – works in village areas. It has three levels:
    *   **Union Parishad** – village level.
    *   **Upazila Parishad** – sub-district level.
    *   **Zila Parishad** – district level.
*   **Urban Local Government** – works in towns and cities. It has two levels:
    *   **Pourashava (Municipality)** – for small towns.
    *   **City Corporation** – for big cities like Dhaka or Chattogram.

---

### 3. Discuss the characteristics of local government in Bangladesh. *(3 marks)*

The main features are:

*   **Constitutional support:** It has a legal base in the Bangladesh Constitution (Articles 59 and 60) and separate acts.
*   **Democratic and elected:** Most members are chosen by local voters through elections.
*   **Three-tier structure:** Rural areas have Union, Upazila, and Zila Parishads.
*   **Central supervision:** It is not fully independent; the central government guides, monitors, and funds it.
*   **Local service focus:** Its main job is local development such as building roads, managing sanitation, and running health centers.

---

### 4. Discuss the advantages of local government in Bangladesh. *(3 marks)*

Local government gives the following benefits:

*   **Local problems solved locally:** Small issues like broken roads or drainage do not need to go to Dhaka; they are fixed nearby.
*   **People’s participation:** Common people can join planning and decision-making for their own area.
*   **Less pressure on central government:** The national government can focus on big issues because local matters are handled at the local level.
*   **Democratic training:** It helps ordinary people learn about elections, leadership, and civic duties from the ground level.

---

### 5. Differentiate between local government and local self-government. *(3 marks)*

Here are the clear differences:

| Local Government | Local Self-Government |
|---|---|
| It is set up by the central government to manage local areas. | It is run by local people with their own will and local choice. |
| The central government keeps strong control over its work. | It enjoys more freedom and less interference from the center. |
| It has both elected members and appointed government officers. | It is mainly managed by fully elected local bodies. |
| It depends mostly on central funds and grants. | It has its own local taxes and more financial independence. |

### 6. Evaluate the upazila system as a unit of local government. *(5 marks)*

**Introduction:**
The Upazila system is the middle level of rural local government in Bangladesh. It sits between the Union Parishad and the Zila Parishad. It was created to bring government services and decision-making closer to the people.

**Evaluation Points:**

*   **Decentralization:** It moves some power from Dhaka to the local level, so local needs are understood better and faster.
*   **Link between levels:** It works as a bridge between Union Parishads and the District administration, making coordination easier.
*   **Development work:** It helps plan and carry out local projects like roads, culverts, health centers, and schools.
*   **Conflict between officials:** There is often a clash between the elected Upazila Chairman and the appointed UNO, which slows down work.
*   **Lack of money and true power:** Upazila Parishads do not get enough funds and still depend on the central government for most decisions, so they cannot act independently.
*   **Political interference:** National politics often enters local affairs, which harms smooth administration.

**Conclusion:**
The Upazila system is a good step for local democracy, but it needs more money, clear power-sharing rules between the Chairman and UNO, and less central control to work properly.

---

### 7. Write down the functions of UNO. *(3 marks)*

UNO stands for **Upazila Nirbahi Officer**. His/her main duties are:

*   **Chief Executive:** He/she is the chief executive officer of the Upazila Parishad and runs daily office work.
*   **Implements projects:** He/she makes sure government development plans and projects are completed on time.
*   **Coordinates departments:** He/she brings together different government offices (health, education, agriculture, police) to work as a team.
*   **Maintains law and order:** He/she works with local police to keep peace and safety in the area.
*   **Advises the Chairman:** He/she gives administrative advice and support to the elected Upazila Chairman.
