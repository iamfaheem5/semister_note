# NeurIPS Cover Letter

> NeurIPS does not strictly require a cover letter for the main track. When one is required (workshop tracks, datasets and benchmarks track, special calls) or when the authors choose to provide one, this snapshot applies. ICML / CVPR share most expectations.

## Tone

- Contribution-led. Conference reviewers do not have time for marketing.
- Concise. Conference letters trend shorter than journal letters.

## Opening

- One sentence stating the contribution (algorithm, theoretical result, dataset, benchmark).
- Name the track (main / datasets-benchmarks / workshops).

## Contribution Framing

- Three concise contributions, each one sentence.
- One quantitative anchor.
- Avoid "broader impact" framing in the letter itself — that belongs in the manuscript's Broader Impact section (required by NeurIPS).

## Journal Fit

- Two sentences connecting to the conference's published call for papers focus areas.
- Reference at most one recent paper from the same venue.

## Declarations (Required for NeurIPS)

- Originality and dual-submission policy compliance (NeurIPS has explicit rules on this).
- Reproducibility checklist confirmation (referenced from manuscript).

## Length

- Hard ceiling: 400 words.

## Common Desk-Reject Triggers

- Dual-submission policy violation (NeurIPS is strict).
- Missing reproducibility checklist confirmation in the cover letter when the manuscript is borderline.
