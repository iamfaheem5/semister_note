# Generic Cover Letter (Fallback)

> Use this template when the target venue does not match any of the specific templates and no venue-specific guidance is available. Suitable for most mid-tier journals and conferences.

## Tone

- Professional, contribution-led, no marketing language.
- Adapt the depth to the target venue: top journal letters should be tighter and more selective; mid-tier journals can include more methodological detail.

## Opening

- First sentence states the manuscript title, type, and central finding.
- Name the target journal or conference.

## Contribution Framing

- Two to three sentences naming the contribution and the headline quantitative result.
- One sentence on what this enables or resolves.

## Journal Fit

- Two sentences explaining why this venue is the right home.
- If possible, reference a recent paper from the same venue.

## Declarations

- Originality: not under concurrent consideration.
- All authors approve the submission.
- Conflicts of interest (state explicitly even when "none").

## Length

- Default ceiling: 400 words. Adjust based on the venue if known.

## Common Desk-Reject Triggers

- Generic "this work is novel and important" with no specific evidence.
- Missing declarations.
- Cover letter that reads as a rephrased abstract.
