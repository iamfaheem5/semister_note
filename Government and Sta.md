
**1. Definition of State**

A state is an organized political community of people living permanently in a fixed territory, having its own government, and possessing supreme power (sovereignty) over its people and land. It is the highest form of political organization that makes laws and controls public affairs without outside control.

---

**2. Elements of State**

There are four basic elements of a state:

*   **Population:** A state must have a certain number of people. Without people, there can be no state.
*   **Territory:** A state must have a fixed land area with clear boundaries.
*   **Government:** This is the machinery or body of persons that directs the affairs of the state and makes rules for the people.
*   **Sovereignty:** This is the supreme and absolute power of the state to make and enforce laws within its territory. No outside force can control it.

---

**3. Difference between State and Government**

| State | Government |
| :--- | :--- |
| It is a **permanent** organization. | It is **temporary** and changes through elections or revolution. |
| It includes the whole community, land, and sovereignty. | It is only the **ruling part** or machinery of the state. |
| It is an **abstract** idea. | It is a **concrete** body of officials. |
| Sovereignty **belongs to the state**. | Government only **exercises** that sovereignty. |
| The state itself cannot be disobeyed. | A bad government can be removed or changed by the people. |

---

**4. Types of Government**

Governments are usually classified as follows:

*   **Unitary Government:** All power is concentrated in the central government. Local bodies get power from the centre. (Example: Bangladesh, UK)
*   **Federal Government:** Power is divided between the central government and regional states/provinces. (Example: USA, India)
*   **Parliamentary Government:** The executive (Prime Minister and Cabinet) is part of the legislature and is answerable to it. The President is ceremonial. (Example: Bangladesh, UK)
*   **Presidential Government:** The executive (President) is separate from the legislature, is directly elected, and is not answerable to parliament. (Example: USA)
*   **Democracy:** Government is run by the people directly or through elected representatives.
*   **Dictatorship:** One person or a small group rules with absolute power without public consent.

---

**5. Preferred Governmental System and Why**

**Parliamentary Democracy** is preferred.

**Reasons:**
*   **Accountability:** The Prime Minister and Cabinet sit in parliament and are directly answerable to the elected representatives of the people.
*   **Responsiveness:** If the government loses support, it can be removed by a vote of no confidence, so it must stay alert to public needs.
*   **Flexibility and Stability:** It combines the law-making and governing functions, which allows quicker decisions and smoother governance compared to systems where the executive and legislature fight each other.

---

**6. Three Organs of Government**

Modern government is divided into three main organs to balance power:

1.  **Legislature:** The law-making body (e.g., National Parliament / Jatiya Sangsad).
2.  **Executive:** The law-enforcing body that runs daily administration (e.g., President, Prime Minister, Cabinet, Ministries).
3.  **Judiciary:** The law-interpreting body that administers justice (e.g., Supreme Court, High Court, lower courts).

---

**7. Functions of the Three Organs**

**Functions of the Legislature:**
*   Makes, amends, and repeals laws.
*   Controls public money—approves the budget and taxes.
*   Oversees the executive by asking questions and debating policies.
*   Represents the voice of the people.
*   Approves treaties and important national appointments.

**Functions of the Executive:**
*   Enforces and executes the laws made by the legislature.
*   Runs the daily administration of the country.
*   Manages foreign affairs, defense, and trade.
*   Appoints important officials and judges.
*   Prepares the national budget and proposes new policies.

**Functions of the Judiciary:**
*   Interprets the constitution and laws.
*   Settles disputes between citizens, and between citizens and the state.
*   Punishes criminals through fair trials.
*   Protects fundamental rights of the people.
*   Uses **judicial review** to cancel any law or action that goes against the constitution.
